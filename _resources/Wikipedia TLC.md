Based on the sources, Wikipedia’s content is organized into the following major **Top-level categories (TLC)**, which represent broad subject areas for browsing:

*   **Culture and the arts**
*   **Geography and places**
*   **Health and fitness**
*   **History and events**
*   **Human activities**
*   **Mathematics and logic**
*   **Natural and physical sciences**
*   **People and self**
*   **Philosophy and thinking**
*   **Reference works** (or **General reference**)
*   **Religion and belief systems**
*   **Society and social sciences**
*   **Technology and applied sciences**

While these 13 subject areas are used for primary navigation, some sources identify a total of **17 top-level categories** that are direct subcategories of the "Fundamental" or "Main topic classifications" categories, including more specific areas such as **Games** and **Matter**.
