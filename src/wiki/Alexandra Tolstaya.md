Alexandra Lvovna Tolstaya (Александра Львовна Толстая; 30 June 1884 NS – 26 September 1979) was the youngest daughter of [[Leo Tolstoy]] and [[Sophia Tolstaya]], his personal secretary in his final years, and a memoirist.

Born at [[Yasnaya Polyana]], she became increasingly close to her father as tensions in the household mounted in his last decade. She served as his secretary, assisting him with correspondence and manuscripts, and shared his views against her mother in the conflict with [[Vladimir Chertkov]]. She was present at [[Astapovo]] at the time of his death in November 1910.

After the Revolution she was imprisoned briefly, then released and emigrated. She settled in the United States, where she founded the Tolstoy Foundation to assist Russian refugees, and wrote several volumes of memoirs about her father that are a primary source for his final years and the events surrounding his departure from Yasnaya Polyana.

She died in 1979 at the age of 95, the longest-lived of Tolstoy's children.
