Astapovo (Астапово; now renamed Lev Tolstoy in honour of the writer) was a small railway station on the Ryazan-Ural line, in what is now Lipetsk Oblast, Russia. It is the site where [[Leo Tolstoy]] died on 7 November 1910 (OS) / 20 November 1910 (NS).

On the night of 27–28 October 1910 (NS), Tolstoy left [[Yasnaya Polyana]] without warning, accompanied by his physician Dushan Makovitsky. He travelled by train towards the south, intending to reach a monastery or find a quiet place to live out his remaining time. On 31 October (NS), he became gravely ill at Astapovo station and could travel no further. He was taken to the house of the station master, Ivan Ivanovich Ozolin, where he spent his final six days.

News of Tolstoy's illness spread rapidly and journalists, followers, and family members gathered at the station. [[Sophia Tolstaya]] arrived but was initially prevented from seeing her husband by those around him. She was admitted to his bedside only shortly before his death.

Tolstoy died of pneumonia on 20 November 1910 (NS). The station was renamed Lev Tolstoy (Лев Толстой) in 1918.
