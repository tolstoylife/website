Pavel Ivanovich Birukoff (Павел Иванович Бирюков; 15 November 1860 NS – 10 October 1931) was a Russian publicist and social activist, a close friend and follower of [[Leo Tolstoy]], and the author of the principal early biography of Tolstoy.

Birukoff met Tolstoy in the 1880s and became part of the inner circle of Tolstoyans alongside [[Vladimir Chertkov]]. He collaborated with Chertkov on the *Posrednik* publishing enterprise and shared exile for his activities on behalf of the Doukhobors.

His four-volume biography, *Leo Tolstoy: His Life and Work* (first published in Russian 1906–1923, translated into English by Maude and others), is one of the primary narrative sources for Tolstoy's life. It draws on Birukoff's personal acquaintance with Tolstoy, correspondence, and access to family papers. Despite its sympathetic perspective, it remains an important source in the project's authority hierarchy.
