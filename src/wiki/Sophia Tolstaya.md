Sofya Andreyevna Tolstaya (Софья Андреевна Толстая, born Bers; 3 September 1844 NS – 4 November 1919) was the wife of [[Leo Tolstoy]], his closest collaborator for nearly five decades, and the principal transcriber of his manuscripts.

Born into the cultivated Bers family in Moscow, she married Tolstoy on 23 September 1862 (NS), when she was eighteen and he was thirty-four. The couple had thirteen children, nine of whom survived infancy. She managed the Yasnaya Polyana household, supervised the education of their children, and handled much of the correspondence and practical affairs of the estate while Tolstoy wrote.

Her contribution to the manuscripts was substantial. She transcribed *War and Peace* by hand multiple times as Tolstoy revised, and performed the same service for *Anna Karenina* and many of his later works. Her copies are primary sources for the textual history of these works.

Sophia kept a diary throughout her married life, beginning shortly after her wedding. Her diaries, which run to many volumes, are an essential primary source for Tolstoy's life and for the deteriorating state of their marriage in his final decades. The conflict between her and [[Vladimir Chertkov]] — who she believed was drawing Tolstoy away from his family — intensified in the last years of Tolstoy's life and contributed to the crisis that preceded his departure from [[Yasnaya Polyana]] in October 1910.

She died at Yasnaya Polyana on 4 November 1919, nine years after her husband, and is buried there.
