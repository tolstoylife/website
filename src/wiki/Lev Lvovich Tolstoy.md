Lev Lvovich Tolstoy (Лев Львович Толстой; 1 June 1869 NS – 18 October 1945) was a son of [[Leo Tolstoy]] and [[Sophia Tolstaya]], and a writer, journalist, and sculptor.

Born at [[Yasnaya Polyana]], he pursued a literary and artistic career and published writing on his own account as well as memoirs and commentary on his father. His relationship with his father was at times strained; he was associated with more conservative positions than those his father espoused in his later years.

He died in 1945, having outlived his father by thirty-five years.
